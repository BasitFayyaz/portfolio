02 — Approach

A few things I keep coming back to.

01

Ship the smallest thing that works

Real users in front of working software beats six weeks of architecture astronautics. Build the spine, see what bends, then harden the parts that actually need it.

02

Boring tech, on purpose

Next.js, Postgres, a queue, a job runner, an error tracker. The stack is intentionally unsurprising so the surprises can live in the product, not the infra.

03

Observability before features

Sentry, structured logs, and a way to replay what happened to a real user. If I can't see a bug, I can't fix it — and dashboards beat hunches every time.

04

AI as a teammate, never the autopilot

I lean on Claude for reply drafts, classification, and codegen — but with humans in the loop, clear guardrails, and an audit trail. No silent auto-sends.
